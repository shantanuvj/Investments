Understanding the Core Risks

Debt Fund: Credit Risk

Credit risk is the chance that a bond issuer might default on payments. Fund managers mitigate this by diversifying across securities with different credit ratings. A higher allocation to 'AAA' rated bonds signifies a more conservative, lower-risk portfolio.

Debt Fund: Interest Rate Risk

Interest rates and bond prices move in opposite directions. When the RBI raises rates, the price of existing bonds (and thus the fund's NAV) tends to fall. This chart illustrates the inverse relationship, which is a key risk for debt fund investors.

The Taxation Showdown (Post-April 2023)

A significant change in tax laws has leveled the playing field between FDs and Debt Funds. For investments made after April 1, 2023, the tax treatment on gains is now identical, removing the previous long-term tax advantage debt funds held.

The Final Verdict: Which is for You?

Choose a Fixed Deposit If...

  • Your #1 priority is capital safety.
  • You want 100% predictable, guaranteed returns.
  • You prefer a simple, hands-off investment.
  • You are highly risk-averse.

Choose a Debt Fund If...

  • You seek potentially higher, market-linked returns.
  • You understand and accept moderate risks.
  • You need high liquidity and flexibility.
  • You want to generate regular income via SWP.
